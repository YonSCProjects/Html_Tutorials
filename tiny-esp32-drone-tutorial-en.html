🚁 Tiny ESP32 Drone

Build Your Own Wi-Fi Controlled Quadcopter

What You'll Build

A mini quadcopter drone controlled from your smartphone through Wi-Fi. It uses:

  • ESP32-C3 SuperMini - The brain (runs code and creates Wi-Fi hotspot)
  • MPU6050 - Motion sensor (detects tilt for auto-stabilization)
  • L9110S drivers - Motor controllers (2 drivers for 4 motors)
  • MT3608 booster - Raises battery 3.7V to 5V for electronics
  • LiPo battery - 600mAh power source (4-6 min flight time)
⏱️ Total Build Time: 4-6 hours

(spread over 2-3 sessions recommended)

Mission 1: Build the Frame

⏱️ 30 min

The frame is made from two popsicle sticks glued in a + shape.

Exercise 1.1: Mark the Centers

  1. Take both popsicle sticks (12cm each)
  2. Measure 6cm from each end - this is the center
  3. Mark the center point on each stick with a pencil

Exercise 1.2: Glue the Cross

  1. Place one stick flat on the table
  2. Place the second stick perpendicular (90°) on top
  3. Align the center marks exactly
  4. Use a paper corner to check the 90° angle
  5. Apply hot glue at the intersection
  6. Hold for 30 seconds until glue sets
  7. Let cure for 10 minutes
Frame Assembly (Top View):
              F (Front)
                 │
                 │
    L ───────────╬─────────── R
   (Left)        │        (Right)
                 │
                 │
              B (Back)

╬ = Glue intersection (center)
Total span: ~12cm tip-to-tip
Use minimal glue - a pea-sized drop is enough. Too much glue adds weight!

Exercise 1.3: Label the Arms

  1. Pick which arm will be "Front" (toward you)
  2. Write F, R, B, L on each arm with a marker
  3. Front and Right will have CW (clockwise) motors
  4. Back and Left will have CCW (counter-clockwise) motors
Always keep the same orientation! The drone won't balance if you mix up the arms.

Exercise 1.4: Drill Motor Holes

  1. Measure 1cm inward from each arm tip
  2. Mark the drilling points
  3. Drill 7mm diameter holes, about 9mm deep
  4. DO NOT drill all the way through!
  5. Clean out wood debris with compressed air or blowing

⏱️ 25 min

Install the 4 motors with correct rotation directions.

Exercise 2.1: Understand Motor Rotation

Quadcopters need motors spinning in specific directions to balance:

Motor Rotation Pattern:
         Motor 1 (Front)
              ⊕
              ↻ CW (Clockwise)
              │
              │
Motor 4  ─────┼───── Motor 2
   ⊕          │          ⊕
 ↺ CCW        │        ↻ CW
              │
              ⊕
         Motor 3 (Back)
           ↺ CCW

Front + Right = CW (↻)
Back + Left = CCW (↺)

Which motors spin clockwise (CW)?

Exercise 2.2: Insert Motors

  1. Take one 8520 (8.5×20mm) motor
  2. Insert it into a hole with the shaft pointing UP
  3. Push until the motor body is halfway into the hole
  4. The motor should fit snugly but not forced
  5. Repeat for all 4 motors
Motor Installation (Side View):
             ↑ Shaft up (for propeller)
┌────────────┼────────────┐
│            ╔═╗          │  ← Frame surface
│            ║ ║          │
│            ║ ║ ← Motor  │
│   Glue → ◯╚═╝◯         │     halfway in
│            hole         │
└─────────────────────────┘

◯ = Apply thin ring of hot glue
Do NOT get glue on the motor shaft or bearings! Only glue around the edge where motor meets wood.

Exercise 2.3: Secure with Glue

  1. Apply a thin ring of hot glue around each motor edge
  2. Make sure glue touches both motor body and wood
  3. Keep glue AWAY from the shaft
  4. Let cure for 24 hours for maximum strength
You can continue with other steps while the glue cures - just be gentle with the frame.
✅ Stop! Spin each motor shaft with your finger:
  • All 4 shafts should spin freely
  • No grinding or resistance
  • Motors don't wobble in their holes

  2. Connect RED probe to MT3608 OUT+ pin
  3. Connect BLACK probe to MT3608 OUT- pin
  4. Connect battery RED to MT3608 IN+
  5. Connect battery BLACK to MT3608 IN-

Exercise 3.2: Adjust to 5.0V

  1. The multimeter shows current output voltage
  2. Use a small screwdriver on the adjustment screw
  3. Turn clockwise (↻) to increase voltage
  4. Turn counter-clockwise (↺) to decrease voltage
  5. Adjust until multimeter reads 5.0V (±0.1V is OK)
  6. Disconnect battery when done
Multimeter reading: 5.00V
(Anywhere from 4.9V to 5.1V is acceptable)

What voltage should the MT3608 output?

Never exceed 5.5V! Higher voltage can damage the ESP32.

Exercise 4.2: Mount ESP32

  1. Apply double-sided tape to the bottom of ESP32
  2. Place at center of frame
  3. USB port should face outward (for easy programming)
  4. Press firmly for 10 seconds

Exercise 4.3: Mount MPU6050 (IMPORTANT!)

  1. Use FOAM TAPE (not regular tape!)
  2. Apply foam tape to bottom of MPU6050
  3. Place behind ESP32
  4. Press gently (don't crush the foam)
You MUST use foam tape for MPU6050! The foam absorbs motor vibrations. Without it, your sensor readings will be noisy and the drone won't stabilize.

Mission 5: Power Wiring

⏱️ 45 min

Connect all power wires. Use RED for positive (+), BLACK for ground (-).

Exercise 5.1: Power Distribution Overview

Complete Power Flow:
Battery (3.7V)
    │
    ├── RED ──→ MT3608 IN+
    └── BLACK → MT3608 IN- (GND)
                    │
                    ├── OUT+ (5V) ──┬──→ ESP32 VIN
                    │               ├──→ L9110S #1 VCC
                    │               └──→ L9110S #2 VCC
                    │
                    └── OUT- (GND) ─┬──→ ESP32 GND
                                    ├──→ L9110S #1 GND
                                    ├──→ L9110S #2 GND
                                    └──→ MPU6050 GND

ESP32 3.3V ──→ MPU6050 VCC (sensor needs 3.3V, not 5V!)

Exercise 5.2: Battery to MT3608

  1. Solder RED wire from battery connector to MT3608 IN+
  2. Solder BLACK wire from battery connector to MT3608 IN-
Always check polarity! RED to +, BLACK to -. Reversed polarity will damage components.

Exercise 5.3: MT3608 to ESP32

  1. Solder RED wire from MT3608 OUT+ to ESP32 VIN pin
  2. Solder BLACK wire from MT3608 OUT- to ESP32 GND pin

Exercise 5.4: MT3608 to L9110S Drivers

  1. MT3608 OUT+ → L9110S #1 VCC (use wire splitter or solder joint)
  2. MT3608 OUT+ → L9110S #2 VCC
  3. MT3608 OUT- → L9110S #1 GND
  4. MT3608 OUT- → L9110S #2 GND
Both drivers connect to the SAME power source (parallel connection). Use wire splitters to split the power.

Exercise 5.5: ESP32 to MPU6050

  1. ESP32 3.3V pin → MPU6050 VCC
  2. ESP32 GND → MPU6050 GND
The MPU6050 needs 3.3V, NOT 5V! Using 5V will damage the sensor. The ESP32 has a built-in 3.3V output - use that.

Exercise 9.2: Identify Propeller Types

CW and CCW propellers have different blade angles:

  • CW (Clockwise): Blade leading edge tilts RIGHT when viewed from above
  • CCW (Counter-Clockwise): Blade leading edge tilts LEFT when viewed from above
Most propeller sets are labeled or color-coded. Check your packaging!

Exercise 9.3: Install Each Propeller

  1. Front motor: Press on CW propeller
  2. Right motor: Press on CW propeller
  3. Back motor: Press on CCW propeller
  4. Left motor: Press on CCW propeller
  5. Push each propeller firmly until seated flush
Propellers must be tight! A loose propeller can fly off during flight - dangerous!

Mission 10: First Flight!

Exercise 10.1: Pre-Flight Safety

  1. Find a clear indoor area (at least 2×2 meters)
  2. Remove breakable objects from the area
  3. Have safety glasses ready (recommended)
  4. Keep fingers away from spinning propellers
Never put fingers near spinning propellers! They can cause cuts.

Exercise 10.2: Power Up

  1. Disconnect USB cable from ESP32
  2. Place drone on a flat surface
  3. Connect battery to MT3608
  4. ESP32 LED should light up

Exercise 10.3: Connect Phone

  1. On your phone, open Wi-Fi settings
  2. Look for network named "Drone_01"
  3. Connect (no password needed)
  4. Open browser and go to: http://192.168.4.1

Exercise 10.4: First Hover

  1. Click the ARM button
  2. SLOWLY move the throttle slider up
  3. At ~30-40%, motors will start spinning
  4. At ~50-60%, drone should lift off
  5. Keep it low (10-20cm) for first test
  6. To land: slowly reduce throttle
  7. Click DISARM after landing
Throttle Levels:
 0%    = Motors OFF
30-40% = Motors spinning (not lifting)
50-60% = Hover height
60-70% = Higher flight (max safe)

Start slow! Increase throttle gradually.

Exercise 10.5: Post-Flight

  1. Reduce throttle to 0%
  2. Click DISARM
  3. Disconnect battery immediately
  4. Check for hot components (warm is OK, hot is not)
  5. Charge battery if needed (don't leave drained)
